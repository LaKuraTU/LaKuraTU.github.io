Gamification as an approach to improve resilience and reduce attrition in mobile mental health interventions: A randomized controlled trial

2. September 2020 | Silja Litvin, Rob Saunders, Markus A. Maier, Stefan Lüttke

Study Overview

General Description

The aim of this study was to research the effects of a gamified mobile mental health intervention on improving resilience in comparison to an active and an inactive control condition over a time of 5 weeks. Secondarily the changes in personal growth, anxiety and psychological wellbeing were also assessed and they checked for the attrition rate.

What was done?

In the app eQuoo users were educated about psychological concepts including emotional bids, generalization, and reciprocity through psychoeducation, storytelling, and gamification.

What were the main results?

The eQuoo test group showed a statistically significant increase in resilience in comparison to both control groups over 5 weeks (F(2,350) = 8.51, p < 0.001, ηp2 = 0.046). Participants also displayed greater increases with the eQuoo App in Psychological Well-Being (F(2,342) = 3.26, p = 0.04, ηp2 = 0.019) and Personal growth (F(2,348) = 5.81, p = 0.003, ηp2 = 0.03). The ANOVA for the Anxiety Score also showed a significant main effect (F(2,342) = 4.972, p = 0.007, ηp2 = 0.03).
For the attrition rate, the odds of remaining in the study after 5 weeks for those who completed the first questionnaire were 3.94 times higher in the test group compared with the control group and 4.08 times higher compared with the waitlist group.

Conclusion

In conclusion this study effectively showed that the gamified app had more increase in resilience than the non-gamified intervention app and the waitlist group. There could also be seen an improvement in all well-being measurements.
